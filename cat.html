CAT/CT Scans

What is a CAT Scan?

A CT and CAT scan are the same thing, and they stand for Computerized tomography. During this test, you lie down on a bed and you slowly move through a large bagel-shaped hole. The CT scan shoots x-ray beams at a specific part of your body and it takes images of different angles. Then, a omputer takes this information and makes cross section pictures. This scan is then used to help the patient and doctors see a better understanding of the patient's body.

Why is it useful?

CT scans are useful in numerous ways. It is used to diagnose many joint problems, bone fractures, heart disease, cancer, infections, tumors, and many more. They dont just diagnose, they can also locate internal bleeding, and it is also used by surgeons to better evaluate and guide surgeries. It can also help docotors know if a treatment is working.
